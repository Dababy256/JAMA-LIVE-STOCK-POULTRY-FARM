🌡️ Heat / Oestrus Detection

Detection Tips: Cattle cycle every 21 days (heat lasts 12–18h). Goats cycle every 21 days (season: Aug–Jan in Uganda). Sheep cycle every 17 days. Signs: restlessness, mounting, vaginal discharge, reduced milk. Best time to mate: 12h after onset of heat.

🪣 Tick & External Parasite Control

Uganda Protocol: Dip or spray cattle every 7–14 days during wet season, every 14–21 days in dry season. Common dips: Triatix (Amitraz), Bayticol (Flumethrin), Supona. Failure to dip leads to East Coast Fever (ECF), Anaplasmosis, and Babesiosis — all major cattle killers in Kwania District. Goats and sheep: treat with Ivermectin or Dectomax injection every 3 months.

🌿 Pasture & Grazing Rotation

Rotation Rule: Never graze same field more than 3–4 weeks continuously. Rest grazed field minimum 4–6 weeks before returning. Overgrazing destroys pasture and increases parasite burden. Divide available land into 3–4 paddocks and rotate systematically.
